      QR factorization

      Mar 04, 20252 min read

      A=QR where:

      • A is m×n
      • R is square upper triangular matrix
      • Q is Orthogonal matrix

      Steps

      Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization

      most intuitive but not stable
      ref: https://kwokanthony.medium.com/important-decomposition-in-linear-algebra-detailed-explanation-on-qr-decomposition-by-classical-3f8f5425915f

      Householder

      ref: https://kwokanthony.medium.com/detailed-explanation-with-example-on-qr-decomposition-by-householder-transformation-5e964d7f7656

      Steps

      1. init R0​=A
      2. for column ai​​ in A, calculate vi​​=ai​​±∣∣ai​​∣∣2​ei​ where e1​ is standard basis. Choose the sign to avoid cancellation.
      3. wi​=∣∣vi​​∣∣vi​​​, Qi​=I−2wi​wiT​ and Ri​=Qi​Ri−1​.
      4. keep this calculation column by column with only consider sub-matrix until Ri​ is upper right triangle matrix.
      5. Q=∏QiT​, R is last result of Ri​.

      2x2 example

      A=[34​72​] v1​​=[34​]±9+16​[10​]=[84​] w1​=64+26​1​[84​] Q1​=I−2w1​w1T​=[−3/5−4/5​−4/53/5​]

      Then

      R1​=Q1​R0​=Q1​A=[−50​−529​−522​​]

      Since R is already in right upper triangle matrix. We are done with R=R1​ and

      Q=Q1T​=[−53​−54​​−54​53​​]
